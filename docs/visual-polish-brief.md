# Mochi Social Visual Polish Brief

This brief keeps visual work focused on the closed Mochirii alpha: one shared 3D guild room, three curated characters, and the shared guild pet Lirabao.

## Visual Goal

The first screen should feel like a cozy Jade Lantern Room: warm dusk light, quiet stone floor, timber beams, hanging lanterns, jade accents, soft fabric, and a gentle place for members to gather around Lirabao.

The room should feel social and readable before it feels large. Players should immediately understand where their character is, where Lirabao is, and how to approach the pet.

## Room Direction

- Keep the layout to one clear room.
- Use a moon gate, lanterns, low seating, timber beams, stone floor, scholar-rock silhouette, and jade details.
- Keep walkable paths open and obvious.
- Keep Lirabao visible from the starting area.
- Keep interaction prompts close to the pet and easy to read.
- Use baked lighting and lightweight materials for smooth browser play.
- Avoid visual clutter that hides players, prompts, or Lirabao.

## Character Direction

- Use three curated character presets only.
- Make silhouettes distinct enough to recognize different members in the same room.
- Keep color swatches soft, readable, and Mochirii-aligned.
- Do not add upload flows or custom image entry points.

## Lirabao Direction

- Lirabao should read as the shared starter pet, not a collectible item.
- Idle should feel calm and present.
- Approach should feel welcoming.
- Happy should feel clearly responsive to a wave or care.
- Care received should feel warm and shared.
- Refresh and resting states should be gentle, not alarming.

## Interface Direction

- Keep the interface quiet and small.
- Use plain labels for character choice, sign-in state, room status, and Lirabao care.
- Show no-real-value copy clearly.
- Keep player-facing UI focused on the room, characters, Lirabao care, and no real value.
- If the room is unavailable, show the playtest paused message instead of opening an old room.

## Acceptance

- The room loads as the Unity 3D room.
- The old runtime does not appear when the Unity room is required.
- Text fits on desktop browser sizes.
- Lirabao and the care prompt are visible and understandable.
- Two testers can visually distinguish their characters.
- Player-facing copy stays simple, Mochirii-branded, and no-real-value.
